## What changed

These are developments dated after January 2026. Treat the underlying conflicts and tariff regime as already known; this skill covers only the newer events and their resolutions.

**Venezuela strike (January 3, 2026).** U.S. forces carried out a large-scale strike on Caracas, bombing the capital and capturing President Nicolás Maduro, who with his wife was flown to New York to stand trial in federal court. Venezuela's defense ministry said 83 people were killed, including Venezuelan and Cuban security personnel and civilians. The administration framed it as a law-enforcement arrest of indicted fugitives under "inherent constitutional authority"; the "Gang of Eight" received no advance briefing. Brazil, Spain, Chile, Colombia, Mexico, Uruguay (Jan. 4 joint statement) and China condemned it; legal scholars cited via FactCheck.org said it violated the UN Charter and required congressional approval. Per the Chicago Council, the operation is linked to keeping rival powers (China took ~80% of Venezuela's crude exports) from accessing Venezuelan oil.

**Withdrawal from 66 international organizations (Jan. 7, 2026).** Trump announced withdrawal from 66 bodies, including the Intergovernmental Panel on Climate Change and the Partnership for Atlantic Cooperation.

**2026 Iran war — Operation Epic Fury (launched February 28, 2026).** The U.S. and Israel launched a major attack on Iran, hitting at least nine cities, during ongoing nuclear negotiations. Iran retaliated with missile/drone strikes on Israel, U.S. bases, and allied Arab states, and closed the Strait of Hormuz. As of March 2026: 13 U.S. service members killed, 200+ wounded, 1,200+ Iranian civilian deaths. The Stimson Center's Christopher Preble called the war "unconstitutional, unwise."

**Iran war wind-down and contested ceasefire (June 2026).**
| Date | Development |
|------|-------------|
| Jun 11 | Trump claimed a "great settlement" and said the U.S. "ended the war"; Iran's foreign ministry called reports of a deal "merely speculation." Trump had earlier vowed to take Kharg Island (~90% of Iran's crude exports). |
| Jun 16 | At the G7, US-Iran wrapped a second day of talks; Vance said Iran would allow nuclear inspections (Tehran did not comment). |
| Jun 21–22 | Trump threatened to "hit Iran very hard again" over Hezbollah in Lebanon and obscenely threatened Iranian negotiators; Iran (again citing a Hormuz closure) walked out, calling talks a "difficult phase." Pakistan/Qatar said technical talks would continue. Susan Rice (Democrat) called the provisional deal a "horrific surrender"; outgoing GOP senator John Cornyn also criticized it. |

**Supreme Court strikes down IEEPA tariffs (Feb. 20, 2026).** The Court ruled IEEPA does not authorize the president to impose tariffs, invalidating the April 2025 "Liberation Day" reciprocal tariffs and the fentanyl-related tariffs on China, Mexico, and Canada. CBP began processing refunds. This was one of four major presidential-power cases this term (others: firing independent agency leaders, Federal Reserve independence, birthright citizenship).

**Tariff reconstruction after the IEEPA ruling.**
| Authority | Status (as of mid-2026) |
|-----------|--------------------------|
| Section 122 (Trade Act of 1974) | 10% across-the-board surcharge on nearly all countries for 150 days; Court of International Trade ruled against it May 7, 2026 (on appeal, still collected); authority expires July 24, 2026 unless extended |
| Section 301 | Two new investigations: structural manufacturing overcapacity (16 countries, >75% of imports) and forced-labor enforcement (60 economies); final determinations expected July 2026 |
| Section 232 | Existing national-security tariffs remain; steel, aluminum, and copper tariffs updated via June 2, 2026 fact sheet |

**ICE mass-detention litigation.** Per POLITICO's database, of 15,500+ federal district-court rulings on ICE detention practices, at least 13,600 went against the administration. Most concern the administration's argument that it can detain anyone eligible for deportation without a hearing. DHS is now led by Markwayne Mullin (successor to Kristi Noem).

**Louisiana v. Callais.** The Court ruled Louisiana's congressional map was an unconstitutional racial gerrymander while upholding the Voting Rights Act's constitutionality; liberal justices in dissent accused the majority of gutting it.

**Other Supreme Court rulings (2026 term):** sided with a Christian therapist against Colorado's conversion-therapy ban (affecting 20+ states); narrowed the federal law barring drug users from gun possession; allowed a NJ crisis-pregnancy clinic to challenge a subpoena on First Amendment grounds; moved Louisiana climate suits to federal court; declined to rule on Etan Patz-related and capital intellectual-disability cases.

**Midterm posture (2026).** Trump is campaigning "like it's 2024," planning near-weekly rallies; Susie Wiles is chief of staff. Campaign Legal Center alleges a coordinated effort to seize ballots/voting machines, purge rolls, and drive partisan redistricting.

## When to use

Use when answering about Trump-administration events, legal rulings, tariff authorities, or foreign conflicts dated after January 2026 — especially the Venezuela strike, the Iran war and its June 2026 ceasefire negotiations, the IEEPA tariff invalidation and the Section 122/301/232 pivot, ICE detention litigation, or 2026 election controversies. Do not use this skill to re-explain pre-2026 baseline policy.

## Examples

- **Q: Are Trump's "Liberation Day" reciprocal tariffs still in force?** No — the Supreme Court ruled on Feb. 20, 2026 that IEEPA does not authorize them, invalidating them along with the fentanyl-related tariffs on China/Mexico/Canada; CBP is processing refunds. The administration shifted to a Section 122 10% surcharge (struck down at trial level, on appeal, expires July 24, 2026) and expanded Section 301/232 actions.
- **Q: Is there a finalized US-Iran peace deal?** As of late June 2026, no. Trump claimed on June 11 the war was "ended," but Iran called a deal "speculation"; talks repeatedly stalled and Iran walked out June 21 after Trump's threats. Pakistan and Qatar said technical talks would continue.
- **Q: Who leads DHS in 2026?** Markwayne Mullin, successor to Kristi Noem.
